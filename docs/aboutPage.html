Frankenstein

Mary Shelley's "Frankenstein" follows the studies of young scientist, Victor Frankenstein, as he creates a living creature from the dead, only to abandon this creature in horror of its appearance. As the novel progresses, Victor's mental state slowly deteriorates as he cannot accept what he has created and recognize that he is responsible for his creature and its actions.

About Our Project

The story of Frankenstein is a widely known story that explores many difficult themes such as consequences of creation, guilt, isolation, madness, and more. The goal of this project is to answer the following research question: How does Victor Frankenstein's mental state deteriorate over time throughout the novel? We will do so by analyzing the themes of words and phrases throughout the book and how the themes change over time.

Marking Up The Text

We divided the text into sections and each team member read through a section and tagged people, places, and analysis words and phrases. The analysis terms fit into themes that we used to analysis the mental state of Frankenstein. These themes include guilt, isolation, ambition, madness, secrecy, consequences of creation, obsession, worry, self disgust, rage, misery, and illness.
